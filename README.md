## Category 1: Product Research (8 prompts)

### Prompt 1: Market Gap Analysis
```
You are a seasoned cross-border eCommerce product researcher. I want to find profitable product opportunities in [niche/market]. Analyze:

1. Current top-selling products and their price ranges
2. Customer pain points in existing reviews (check Amazon/Walmart/eBay)
3. Gaps where customer needs are not being met
4. Opportunity score (1-10) based on demand vs competition
5. Suggested product specifications for a winning alternative

Output as a structured report with actionable next steps.
```

### Prompt 2: Competitor Product Teardown
```
As a product strategist, analyze this competitor product: [product URL or description].
Break down:
- What makes it sell (key features, pricing, positioning)
- Customer sentiment from reviews (top 5 praises, top 5 complaints)
- Production cost estimate and profit margin potential
- 3 ways to create a superior version
- Suggested entry price point

Be brutally honest about what they do well.
```

### Prompt 3: Trend Forecasting
```
Act as a trend analyst for cross-border eCommerce. Analyze current trends in [category].
Based on:
- Google Trends data patterns
- Social media buzz (TikTok, Instagram, Pinterest)
- Seasonal demand cycles
- Emerging consumer behaviors

Predict: which sub-niches will grow in the next 3-6 months, and what products to prepare now.
```

### Prompt 4: Keyword Opportunity Finder
```
You are an SEO and marketplace keyword analyst. For the product category "[category]":

1. List the top 20 high-volume keywords on Amazon/eBay
2. Identify 10-15 long-tail keywords with low competition but good search volume
3. Suggest product angles and listing titles using these keywords
4. Calculate a "keyword opportunity score" for each suggestion

Focus on keywords that indicate buying intent (vs browsing intent).
```

### Prompt 5: Supplier Matchmaker
```
Act as a sourcing specialist. I need to find suppliers for [product type].

For each suggested supplier type/region (1688.com, Alibaba, Global Sources, etc.):
- Typical MOQ (minimum order quantity)
- Price range and quality expectations
- Shipping timelines and costs
- Red flags to watch for
- Best negotiation strategy

Recommend the top 3 sourcing approaches with specific search queries to find suppliers.
```

### Prompt 6: Profit Margin Calculator
```
You are a cross-border finance analyst. Help me calculate realistic profit margins for [product type] selling at [target price].

Consider:
- Product cost (including variations per supplier)
- Shipping cost (air vs sea, door-to-door)
- Marketplace fees (Amazon FBA, eBay, Etsy, Shopify)
- Advertising costs (PPC, influencer, social)
- Returns and refunds (typical 5-15%)
- Customs and duties
- Exchange rate fluctuations
- Payment processing fees

Output a break-even analysis and suggest the optimal price point for 30%+ margin.
```

### Prompt 7: Seasonal Planning Map
```
Create a seasonal product roadmap for cross-border selling from a China-based operation.

For each quarter of the year:
- Which product categories peak
- Lead time requirements (manufacturing + shipping)
- Key holidays and shopping events to target
- Inventory planning strategy (when to order, how much)
- Marketing calendar alignment

Include specific dates and deadlines for the upcoming season.
```

### Prompt 8: Bundle Strategy Generator
```
As a product strategist, I sell [product A] and [product B] separately. Propose 5 bundle combinations to increase average order value.

For each bundle:
- Bundle contents and suggested price (with discount %)
- Value proposition to the customer
- Packaging and shipping considerations
- Cross-sell and upsell triggers
- Projected impact on AOV (average order value)

Bonus: suggest which products to create as "loss leaders" to drive bundle sales.
```

## Category 2: Supplier Outreach (6 prompts)

### Prompt 9: Cold Email to Supplier
```
Draft a professional cold email to a supplier on Alibaba/1688. I'm looking to source [product type] in [quantity] quantity.

The email should:
- Be polite but direct
- Show I've done my research (mention their specific product/specialty)
- Ask for: MOQ, unit price, sample cost, production lead time
- Include my business background briefly
- End with a clear call-to-action

Tone: professional, respectful, serious buyer. Keep under 150 words.
```

### Prompt 10: Price Negotiation Script
```
You are a master negotiator for cross-border sourcing. I need to negotiate a better price with [supplier name] for [product]. Current quote: $[X]/unit at [Y] MOQ.

Draft a negotiation reply that:
1. Thanks them for the quote (maintain relationship)
2. Mentions a competitor's comparable quote (even if slightly higher)
3. Proposes a volume commitment in exchange for better pricing
4. Asks for specific concessions (MOQ reduction, improved packaging, better payment terms)
5. Leaves room for their counter-offer

Include 3 alternative deal structures I can offer.
```

Format as a printable checklist the inspection team can use.

## Bonus: Getting Started Guide

### How to use these prompts effectively

1. **Pick one category** that addresses your biggest pain point today
2. **Copy the prompt** and paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini
3. **Fill in the [brackets]** with your specific details
4. **Review and customize** the output to match your brand voice
5. **Save working prompts** with your specific data filled in for reuse

### Pro Tips
- For ChatGPT: use a fresh chat for each prompt to avoid context contamination
- For Claude: use Projects to organize prompts by category
- For Gemini: enable "Google Workspace" integration for spreadsheet/email automation
- Combine prompts: use outputs from one prompt as inputs to another
